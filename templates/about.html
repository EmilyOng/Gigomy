The Gig Economy
Our economy is taking a big hit...But with things still unfolding, we knew we might have to do more. The situation is especially serious for some sectors - hotels, aviation, hospitality, and freelancers in the
gig economy
. But nobody has been spared. Everyone feels the impact, to different degrees.
- PM Lee Hsien Loong, on the Covid-19 outbreak
  • Rationale
  • Technical Details

One of the most significant impact of the Covid-19 virus is its impact on the economy, with many people losing their jobs. Unemployment refers to a situation where people of the legal working age are actively looking for work, but are unable to find work.


In the short run, this would lead to a loss of income, which reduces the purchasing power of people, and enable them to buy lesser goods and services (including necessities!) to satisfy their needs and wants. This could lead them to have a lower standard of living.


On a larger scale, unemployment leading to a less productive workforce can make Singapore less attractive as an investment destination, which is detrimental to the future growth of Singapore in the long run. Furthermore, long-term unemployment can lead to hysteresis, which is a loss of skills, and can make it harder for the unemployed to return to the workforce.


While I acknowledge that taking up ad-hoc jobs through Gigomy is not a direct solution to solving unemployment, as people are afterall, working below their productive capacity, it serves as a temporary short-term source of income; This is especially important as they can then use this additional income as means to alleviate the current lack of income to purchase necessities, allowing them more time and income to be directed towards up-skilling themselves, making it easier to transform to productive labour when the Covid-19 situation ends.
